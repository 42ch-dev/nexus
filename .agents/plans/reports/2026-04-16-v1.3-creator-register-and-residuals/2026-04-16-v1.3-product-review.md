## Executive Summary

**Approve** — the V1.3 scope is well-bounded, the wave decomposition is pragmatically sequenced (feature-first, then severity-driven residual batches), and the 35 residual items have clear fix/accept classification criteria. The challenge-solver design is sound and the dependency on platform M10 is properly gated. Two areas warrant discussion before implementation kicks off: (1) the four open design decisions (D1-D4) in the challenge-solver design should be resolved to "Decided" status before Wave 1 T1 starts, and (2) the residual governance should explicitly track which ~8-10 "low" items are Fix vs Accept in Wave 3, since the compass currently lumps them. These are manageable preconditions, not blockers.

## Recommendations

1. **Resolve D1-D4 before Wave 1 starts.** Tentative decisions (CLI-only module, PlatformClient as nexus-sync module, daemon-first storage, single auto-retry) are reasonable defaults — formalize them to "Decided" to prevent mid-wave design churn.

2. **Deduplicate residuals before Wave 3.** Items W-003 (slug collision) and S-006 (experience dup) are duplicates of pipeline R10 and R4 respectively. Consolidate to avoid fixing the same issue twice. Update `status.json` residual entries to reflect deduplication (mark one as `duplicate` lifecycle and close).

3. **Add 4 acceptance criteria** as identified above: LLM fallback graceful degradation, challenge expiry handling, wrong-answer retry limit, and residual close evidence. These fill gaps in the current compass §8 without expanding scope.

4. **Create a Wave 3 fix/accept manifest before starting.** The compass §4 Wave 3 lumps 17 low items together. Before implementation, produce a specific checklist identifying which 13 are Fix and which 4 are Accept, matching the table above. This prevents ambiguity during execution.

5. **Gate Wave 2 on Wave 1 completion, but allow Wave 3-typed fixes to proceed in parallel if independent.** The 4 medium residuals (R8 session capture, R1 should_degrade, R2 placeholder world_id, R3 last_upgrade_attempt) are in different modules from the creator register flow and could be addressed concurrently with Wave 1 T4-T6 if the team capacity allows. Note this requires careful branch coordination per the single-track strategy in compass §2.
