## Goal

Build a **SOUL personality visualization** layer over the creator's internalized memory fragments: (a) **keyword clusters** (what themes the creator has internalized) and (b) **temporal drift** (how those themes shift over time, with growth-count folded into the timeline). Expose the already-stored `keywords` + `created_at` over the wire via an additive `memory-fragment-info` DTO extension. Sparse-data empty states handled gracefully.

### E. User stories

The SOUL visualization serves one core human need — seeing who you are becoming as a creative. Every story below is verifiable against the shipped surface with real fragment data and with a new-creator (zero-fragment) scenario.

- **See my creative themes** — *As an author*, I can look at a keyword cluster visualization of my internalized memory fragments and immediately see what themes my creative work has accumulated — "historical fiction", "moral ambiguity", "ensemble casts", "slow-burn romance" — so I understand what my AI assistants and I have been focusing on, even if I haven't consciously tracked it. The clusters surface the *top* themes, not a raw dump of every low-frequency keyword.
- **See how my focus shifts over time** — *As an author*, I can see a temporal-drift timeline that shows how my keyword composition has changed — which themes rose (e.g., "political intrigue" emerging late in a Work), which faded — and how much memory I've accumulated over time (fragment count folded in). This gives me the "seeing who I am becoming" insight: am I drifting toward or away from the kind of writer I want to be?
- **Start from nothing with confidence** — *As a new author* with zero accumulated fragments, I see a graceful, encouraging state that explains what this visualization *will* show and what actions will populate it. The copy is empathetic and forward-looking: it must not feel like a broken feature or a bug. I understand that "my SOUL is just beginning" and I know what to do next (keep writing and reviewing).
- **Low-data dignity** — *As an author* with just a few fragments, the viz degrades gracefully: a simple frequency list or word cloud is more honest than a forced cluster chart with one node. The transition from "not enough data" → "simple view" → "rich clusters" is seamless and never renders broken or misleading visualizations.

These stories define product success for Track B. The "seeing who you are becoming" value is the primary differentiating insight — keyword clusters without the temporal dimension would miss the core product proposition. The temporal drift timeline is what makes this a **reflection tool**, not a tag cloud.

### F. Sparse-data empty state — product copy guidance

The SOUL viz must handle three data-density states with appropriate visual treatment and empathetic copy:

| State | Condition | Visual treatment | Copy (empathetic, encouraging) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Empty** | Zero fragments (`list-fragments` returns `[]`) | Soft illustration or icon set; no chart rendered. Full-width centered layout with a forward-looking CTA. | "Your SOUL is just beginning. As you write and review, Nexus captures fragments of your creative identity — themes, patterns, obsessions — and maps them here. Come back after your first review session." |
| **Low-data** | 1–20 fragments; too few for meaningful clusters | Simple keyword frequency list (not a forced cluster chart). Fragment count shown prominently. Timeline shows sparse but honest data points — no interpolation or misleading curves. | "Your SOUL is taking shape. {N} fragments captured so far — enough to see early themes. Keep writing and reviewing to build richer patterns." (where {N} is the live fragment count) |
| **Rich** | 21+ fragments; clusters are meaningful | Full keyword clusters + temporal drift timeline with fragment-count fold-in. Interactive: hover for counts, click a keyword to filter fragments. | No guidance copy needed — the data speaks. Optional subtitle: "Your creative themes over time." |

**Anti-patterns to avoid** (these would erode trust in the feature):
- Rendering a chart with one data point (looks broken).
- Showing "0 results" as an error-state (implies failure, not beginnings).
- Using placeholder/lorem-ipsum data that misleads the author about their actual SOUL content.
- Any copy that implies the author "failed" to generate enough data ("you don't have enough fragments yet" → wrong tone; the author hasn't done anything wrong).
- An empty state that is silent — the author should leave understanding what the feature is, not wondering if it's broken.
