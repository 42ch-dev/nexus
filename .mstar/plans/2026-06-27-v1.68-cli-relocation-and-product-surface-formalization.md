## Context

`nexus42` is the product (CLI + integrated daemon runtime); the other `crates/*` are its libraries. Today the product binary is buried among 15 library crates, and `apps/` — the dir contributors open for "what can I run" — lists only the TS clients and omits the CLI. V1.68 relocates `crates/nexus42` → `apps/nexus42` to unify the **"all product surfaces under `apps/`"** mental model, returns `crates/` to pure libraries, and pins the placement rule in docs. Low-risk: Cargo resolves intra-workspace deps by crate **name** → zero source changes; CI references crate names → CI-invisible.

## Notes

- **Why zero source changes**: intra-workspace Cargo deps use the crate *name* (`nexus42`), resolved via the workspace; the `members` list is the only Cargo.toml that tracks the *path*. Moving the dir + updating that one line is sufficient.
- **Why pnpm is safe**: `pnpm-workspace.yaml` globs `apps/*`, but pnpm only treats dirs containing `package.json` as packages; `apps/nexus42` (pure Rust) has none → ignored.
- **Why `archived/` is untouched**: those records (legacy `nexus42d`, closed residuals, historical design docs) are point-in-time snapshots; rewriting their paths falsifies the audit trail. They age out naturally.
