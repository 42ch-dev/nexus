### F-QC2-04: `tokio::sync::Mutex` for `start_handles` — RESOLVED ✅

**Fix verification:**
- `crates/nexus42d/src/lifecycle/actions.rs`: `start_handles` field changed from `tokio::sync::Mutex<Vec<JoinHandle<()>>>` to `std::sync::Mutex<Vec<JoinHandle<()>>>`.
- `enter_starting()`: Changed from `ctx.start_handles.try_lock()` with warning fallback to `ctx.start_handles.lock()` (std Mutex doesn't have try_lock without parking_lot).
- `exit_starting()`: Changed from `ctx.start_handles.blocking_lock()` to `ctx.start_handles.lock().expect("start_handles mutex not poisoned")`.
- **Correctness analysis:** `std::sync::Mutex` is appropriate here because the mutex is held only for brief push/drain operations (no `.await` while holding the lock). This eliminates the tokio::sync::Mutex overhead for a synchronously-used resource.

**Assessment:** Correct fix. The mutex is used synchronously (lock → push/drain → drop) in both entry and exit actions, making `std::sync::Mutex` the right choice.
