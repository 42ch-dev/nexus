### D. Clarify gates (converged — product decisions locked)

All three clarify gates are **converged** with product rationale. The `@product-manager` confirms the recommendations; no recommendation is overridden.

1. **Reading surface profile scope → novel-first (CONFIRMED).** Product rationale: the reading surface primarily serves novelists — the profile most likely to have long-form prose that benefits from in-context re-reading with maturation context. Essays, game-bibles, and scripts benefit from the same body render (profile-agnostic) on day one. Profile-specific reading chrome (e.g., essay section-break styling, game-bible entity cross-reference overlays) would be speculative without usage data from the novel-first launch. Deferred, not rejected — other profiles gain reading-surface value immediately from the body render alone. The maturation indicators are also profile-agnostic (chapter status, KB density, findings count).
2. **SOUL viz scope → per-creator (CONFIRMED).** Product rationale: SOUL fragments are `creator_id`-scoped in the `memory_fragments` table — per-creator is the natural scope, reflecting "one author's internalized creative self." Per-World filtering is a valuable refinement but introduces a UI dimension (World picker, cross-World comparison) that would delay the MVP. A creator writing multiple Worlds sees their blended SOUL, which is itself an insightful data point ("how do my worlds influence each other, thematically?"). Per-World filter is a deferred enhancement, not a gap — the blended view has independent product value.
3. **Reading progress → session-only (CONFIRMED).** Product rationale: persisted progress requires a write route (breaking the read-only surface invariant) and a new persistence table. The product value of "resume where I left off across sessions" is real but not urgent for V1.79 — authors using the reading surface session-to-session can navigate to their current chapter in one click (chapter nav is always visible). Persisted progress is deferred with a tracker row, not rejected. If post-V1.79 usage shows authors using the reading surface as a primary consumption tool across sessions, it becomes a high-priority V1.80 candidate.

**Decision**: all three gates are LOCKED with the recommendations adopted. No further clarify iteration needed. P0 and P1 plans may treat these decisions as binding scope.
