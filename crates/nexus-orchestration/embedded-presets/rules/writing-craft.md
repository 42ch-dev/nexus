# Writing Craft Rules — Shared Layer 1

> Immutable per version; user override: `~/.nexus42/rules/writing-craft.md`

This document provides cross-Work craft guidance read by all `novel-writing` runs.

## Prose Quality

- Prefer concrete sensory details over abstract statements.
- Vary sentence length: short for impact, long for immersion.
- Active voice is default; passive only when the receiver matters more than the actor.

## Scene Craft

- Open each scene with a hook (action, dialogue, sensory detail, or mystery).
- Ground the reader in time and place within the first two paragraphs.
- End scenes on a turn: new information, emotional shift, or unresolved tension.

## Pacing

- Alternate between high-tension and recovery scenes.
- A chapter should change at least one character's situation or understanding.
- Avoid travelogue passages that narrate transit without conflict or revelation.

## Consistency

- Track character speech patterns, quirks, and knowledge state.
- Maintain temporal logic: cause before effect, information before use.
- World rules established early must hold unless a justified in-world exception is introduced.

## Five-Question Gate (五問)

Before finalizing any chapter, verify:

1. **Does the scene advance the plot or character arc?**
2. **Is the prose clear and free of unintentional ambiguity?**
3. **Are character voices distinct and consistent?**
4. **Does the ending create forward momentum?**
5. **Is the world internally consistent within this scene?**

If any answer is "no," revise before marking the chapter as finalized.
