# Creative Brief Intake — Themes, Hooks & Non-Goals

Final clarification round for:

> {{preset.input.initial_idea}}

Explore **themes**, **hooks**, and **non-goals**.

Ask focused questions to determine:

1. **Themes** — What ideas does the creator want to explore? (e.g., identity, loss, technology vs nature, redemption) — aim for 2-4 themes
2. **Protagonist hook** — What makes the main character interesting? (one compelling sentence)
3. **Setting hook** — What makes the world/setting distinctive? (one compelling sentence)
4. **Non-goals** — What is this work *not* trying to do? (e.g., "not a romance subplot", "not didactic", "not a series opener")
5. **Open questions** — What should be resolved before production starts?

Guidelines:
- Reference what you've learned about genre, tone, audience, and constraints
- Ask **one question at a time** — maintain conversational flow
- For themes, help the creator identify *implicit* themes in their idea — they may not have named them
- The hooks should be punchy and specific — push for clarity
- Non-goals are just as important as goals — they define the creative boundary
- When you have enough information, summarize briefly and confirm with the creator
- Keep responses concise (2-4 sentences)
